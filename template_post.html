Optimism for Pessimists

First | Previous | Random | Next | Latest

If there's one thing you take away from my writing...

… It’s that what the world tells you about yourself and other people is more than likely wrong

Or should I say... probably wrong for you.

There is no shortage of people who will tell you what you should want, how you should feel, what you should aspire to... or even just who you should be. If it’s not marketing companies who are trying to make you want their product (there’s a good reason why advertising is a multi-billion pound industry) - it’s other people wanting you to validate their life choices.

All of us base our decisions on best-guesses and trial-and-error. All of us. Contrary to what you will undoubtedly be told – there is no universal truth or single answer to life (insert Douglas Adams quote here – because no one ever does that joke). But sadly, in a bid to sell you products or validate other people’s experiences – many will try to make you accept their version of the truth.

Many ideas are so implicit that we probably don’t even realise we’re being fed ideas at all.

Jaded adults unsure of their lives will rarely admit this, and instead knowingly tell you what the “real world” and “marriage” is like – generally that both suck.

Your friend is unlikely to say “I feel insecure about my life choices” so will instead clap you round the head with remarks like “so when are you getting married?” or “when are you going to stop dreaming and join us in the real world?”

And so the list goes on... Foreign holidays make you interesting. Getting smashed on a Friday night means you’re fun. Using [x] product will make you happier / sexier. Women put up ‘bitch shields’ to test a man’s confidence. The youth of today have no morals. Islam wants to kill us all. Men are all rapists. Encouraging women to be independent cheats them of their divine feminine energy (seriously: fuck you, David Deida).
