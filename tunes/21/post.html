Optimism for Pessimists

Tidalis [Piano Cover]

  • Original by:

    Pablo Vega

  • Game:

    Tidalis

  • Heard in:

    Game's title screen (where I instantly fell in love with it)

DVD Extras:

If there is such a thing as a 'Happy Song', this would be it. Hands down.

I was captivated by the game, Tidalis, immediately on the loading screen where I was greeted by a boat on the waves and this tune. It was mid-winter in the UK at the time, cold and dark, so this was basically an inviting escape to a warm, tropical island.

Perhaps strangely, then, I have guilt to thank for this piece more than anything.

To tell the truth, I had been wanting to play this song for years. As the original is performed on a xylophone, it wasn't too difficult to picture how it could sound on the piano. I just never had the guts to give it a try. There was no sheet music, so I'd have to figure it all out by myself. It was always one of those things that I earmarked as "I'd like to try that when I'm better" which, as anyone else who's made similar pacts will know, generally means "never".

That was until I was nearing completion of the second compilation of piano covers. I like to arrange my recordings into albums, mainly as I hate having singles floating about my iTunes, masquerading as albums. I like to get swept away on a little journey with each an album, rather than be presented with a few sweets and shown the door.

As my second collection was entirely computer games covers, I had already settled upon the name Hollow Tides in my head, in a homage to Hollow Knight and Tidalis. I liked the ring to it, probably because I'm a sucker for anything with an aquatic twinge to it (except cruises. There's something deeply unsettling about being trapped on a vessel with children's entertainers and shuffleboard enthusiasts).

However, with two songs left to complete and no sign of Tidalis anywhere in the album yet, I had to face a difficult choice: Change the title or suck it up and learn the damn pieces. I couldn't call it Hollow Tides and not have Tidalis music in there. That's just dumb and silly and dumb.

Whilst a trite thing to labour over, Hollow anything else just didn't sound as good. Not now that I'd already settled on the name. Everything else was pants in comparison. Hollow Chocobo screams more of a budget horror movie than an ode to computer games.

The second thing to push me over the edge was looking back at all of the recordings and realising that I only had one song learned by ear. Considering I had somewhere in the region of 10 years of piano under my belt, I confess I felt a bit sheepish at only having one attempt actually recorded, especially as I'd come out with quite a few back in my third year or so. Whilst there was obvious merit in learning songs directly from the sheet music (especially where I was still trying to figure out the sort of sound I wanted), I also realised that I was putting off trying at all.

I looked back at my promise of "when I was better" and figured that if 10 years wasn't good enough to give it a shot, then I was never going to come close.

And the rest is... well. You can see it above.

And that is how a bizarre blend of unwavering continuity and guilt kicked the ass of nervousness. Whilst they're not your regular superheroes, I have them to thank for finally snagging me this piece.
