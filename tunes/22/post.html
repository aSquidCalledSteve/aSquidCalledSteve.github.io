Optimism for Pessimists

Adventure Intro [Piano Cover]

  • Original by:

    Pablo Vega

  • Game:

    Tidalis

  • Heard in:

    Intro to the game's single-player story mode

DVD Extras:

Given the name "Adventure Intro", it seems strange that this is one is the finale of the second compilation of covers. In a curious twist of irony though, its naming and position became quite apt.

Having wrapped up the second collection in what felt like a neat bow, the obvious question was now what the third collection should look like. I had flirted with the idea that the third being the one where I basically re-learned all of the pieces I'd previously forgotten and finally get some decent recordings of them down.

A few songs in and I started to run into a problem, however. My musical style had changed. The computer game covers, especially the pair of closing Tidalis tracks, had sparked a very different sound for the piano for me. One that I enjoyed considerably more than the chord-thumping of yester-year. Pop songs (at least in the way I played them at the time) were grudgingly just no longer hitting the spot. I had a taste of the sound I wanted from the piano, and my style now had to play catch-up. Curiously, it was whilst writing up these DVD Extras that I really started to sit down and think about what I wanted from the piano. What did I want to be sitting with in a years time?

There were a number of pieces I was eyeing up but sidelining for compilation number four or five... But then I thought "why am I waiting? Why am I purposefully not playing the pieces I have the most interest in?"

Around that time, I was also very fortunate to start finding a few interesting albums (which will be topics of their own) and piano musicians that crafted interesting covers that played more to the piano's strength. I had an angle that I previously thought impossible and additional music theory in the jazz/blues sphere that I realised I should probably pay attention to.

And then I emigrated. That kinda threw a spanner into the works too. Away from my trusty Arius, I found myself in the company of a baby grand piano in a church hall for my practice. I was woefully unprepared for the incredible sound it produced though. Imagine playing a xylophone where every note you tap sets off a stick of dynamite beneath your seat. That was my experience with the baby grand. Most of the pieces carefully recorded and honed were now sounding explosively terrible when amplified. I thought the jump from keyboard to Arius was bad... This was something else, to the point that I initially played with ear plugs until I got used to playing with such a volatile instrument. On top of new ideas and new theory, I might as well have been sat at an entirely new instrument. It appeared that I had much to learn and re-learn. It was glorious.

And so it was, that as I concluded my second compilation, the adventure was indeed only just beginning.
