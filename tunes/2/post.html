Fisherman's Horizon [Piano Cover]

  • Original by:

    Nobuo Uematsu

  • Game:

    Final Fantasy VIII

  • Heard in:

    Fisherman's Horizon, where I'd love to holiday were it not for the almost guaranteed permanent smell of fish

Dedication: Game soundtracks

DVD Extras:

This is the one to blame for my slippery slope into game soundtracks (that's a slight lie. I made a cassette of game music I liked from my Mega Drive when I was about 8).

I was replaying Final Fantasy VIII as a teenager - this time armed with a spreadsheet of the ultimate GF abilities and combos. I had arrived at Fisherman's Horizon with one obvious objective in mind: Getting more trading cards (which is more important than saving the world in these games).

What I vividly remembered, however, was being utterly captivated by the music. Maybe being an angsty teen with a chip on my shoulder the size of Montana made me more prone to soothing music, but it was one of those rare tracks that just appeared to encapsulate the very concept of serenity. I remember laying on my back in bed and just taking my sweet time walking around so I could enjoy this piece a little bit more.

So when it came to picking pieces to start learn... this was a complete no-brainer. This piece was also why I started recording in the first place. I confess, I actually had to learn this twice. For whatever reason, I stopped practising this song at some point many years ago and discovered that, much to my dismay, I had forgotten how to play it. Not wanting to lose more pieces to antiquity, I decided it was high time to stop being flippant and dedicate myself to learning pieces properly and keeping them there.
